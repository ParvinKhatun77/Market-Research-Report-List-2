<p><p>Trail mountain bikes are categorized into four main market types based on frame materials: Carbon Fiber, Aluminum, Titanium, and Alloy. Carbon Fiber frames are lightweight and provide superior strength, offering excellent performance but at a higher price. Aluminum frames are affordable and durable, making them popular among entry-level riders. Titanium frames combine lightweight properties with exceptional durability, but they tend to be costlier. Alloy frames, often a mix of different metals, offer a balance between weight, strength, and affordability, appealing to a broad audience.</p></p>
