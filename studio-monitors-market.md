<p><p>The studio monitors market primarily consists of two types: active monitors and passive monitors. Active monitors feature built-in amplifiers, allowing for straightforward setup and optimal sound reproduction. They are favored for their convenience and efficiency. In contrast, passive monitors require external amplifiers, offering flexibility in sound customization but demanding more technical knowledge for setup. Both types cater to different user needs, with active monitors often preferred by home studios, while passive monitors appeal to professional settings needing tailored audio configurations.</p></p>
