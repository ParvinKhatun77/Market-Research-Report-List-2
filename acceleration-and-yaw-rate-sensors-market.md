<p><p>The acceleration and yaw rate sensors market comprises two primary types: piezoelectric and micromechanical sensors. Piezoelectric sensors detect changes in acceleration by converting mechanical stress into an electrical charge, making them suitable for high-frequency applications. In contrast, micromechanical sensors, often based on MEMS technology, leverage tiny mechanical elements to measure acceleration and rotation, offering compact designs and high sensitivity. Both sensor types are crucial in automotive, aerospace, and consumer electronics for stability control, navigation, and motion detection.</p></p>
