<p><p>Positron Emission Tomography (PET) systems comprise three main market types: Direct Imaging, Indirect Imaging, and Alternative Imaging. Direct Imaging involves capturing gamma rays emitted from positron decay, providing high-resolution images for cancer and neurological assessments. Indirect Imaging uses a combination of scintillation crystals and detectors to enhance image sensitivity, often in multicenter studies. Alternative Imaging encompasses emerging technologies like hybrid imaging systems, including PET/MRI and PET/CT, improving diagnostic capabilities by integrating metabolic and anatomical information for better patient outcomes.</p></p>
