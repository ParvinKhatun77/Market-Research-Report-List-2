<p><p>The Large-Joint Reconstructive Implants market comprises three main types: metallic, ceramic, and polymers. Metallic implants, often made from titanium or cobalt-chromium alloys, are known for their strength and durability, making them suitable for high-load-bearing applications. Ceramic implants offer biocompatibility and wear resistance, providing a smoother surface for joint movement. Polymers, typically used in conjunction with other materials, are lightweight and flexible, aiding in shock absorption, which enhances patient comfort. Together, these materials cater to various reconstructive needs in orthopedic surgeries.</p></p>
